Introduction

The simplest story to tell about SUNY Geneseo's Writing Learning Center (WLC) over the last year is the story of growth. The WLC, to get right to the point, had more client interactions over the course of the 2014-2015 school year than any other in its history, having recorded 1,913 client interactions from September 2014 to May 2015. These nearly 2,000 client interactions mark a triumphant success for the Writing Learning Center in moving to an online client-driven model for making appointments with writing tutors. Now, more than ever, clients are empowered to seek out and obtain the help they feel they need when it comes to writing.

School Year Client Interactions
2010-2011 647
2011-2012 664
2012-2013 885
2013-2014 00
2014-2015 1,913

Of course, a 116% increase in client interactions over two years means little without some context for the growth. If our clients are simply rushing to come in when key assignments or finals are due, the growth rate would be substantially less impressive. Such a client pattern would, in fact, indicate a distinct lack of core value in the WLC, as it would suggest that students mainly used it as a final method for proofreading, not a service for consistent learning and skill improvement. However, the incredible growth of the WLC has not been so superfluous. The data collected over the 2014-2015 school year indicate that students are consistently accessing the WLC over the course of the semester and that there is no exceptional increase in interactions because of finals or midterms.
